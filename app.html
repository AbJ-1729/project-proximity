The App

Simple to use.
Serious underneath.

On the surface it is one switch and a short list of people nearby. Underneath is a privacy model designed so the system learns as little about you as possible while still being genuinely useful.

The everyday experience

  • Sign in with your phone number on Android. Pick the people from your contacts you would actually want to run into.
  • They are only told you picked them if they pick you back. No notifications, no pressure, no one-sided exposure.
  • When you feel social, flip discovery on and optionally set a vibe like "coffee free" or "studying".
  • Anyone from your circle who is in the same place and also discovering shows up, with their name from your own contacts and a rough closeness like "very close".
  • Turn discovery off and you immediately stop being discoverable. Block anyone, anytime, in one tap.


1ST DEGREE

Friends show as themselves

A direct friend appears with the name you saved for them, their current vibe, and a coarse proximity. That name comes from your phone, not our servers.

2ND DEGREE

Friends of friends stay private

Opt in to a wider circle and a friend of a friend appears only as "Friend of Sam", the person you both know. Their name is never shown. You get introduced in person, the natural way.



Under the hood

The privacy engineering

For the technically curious and anyone evaluating the product seriously, here is how the guarantees are actually enforced.

On-device hashing
Contacts never leave your phone in readable form. Numbers are normalized and hashed with SHA-256 plus a shared pepper on the device, so the server only ever sees opaque hashes, never your phonebook.
Ephemeral presence
Real-time presence lives only in an in-memory store with a 15-minute time to live. There is no persistent location history; expired check-ins are evicted automatically and never written to the main database.
Proof of presence
Co-location is confirmed by what two phones can physically sense of each other, not by trusting a GPS coordinate: the set of nearby Wi-Fi access points they share, and a direct Bluetooth signal between them for places with no Wi-Fi at all, like a beach or a park. The Bluetooth check requires both phones to sense each other, which is strong, hard-to-spoof evidence of being in the same place. Your precise location never leaves the phone.
One-way block table
Blocks are enforced inside the matching query itself. A blocked person can neither match you directly nor bridge a friend-of-a-friend path to you, closing the usual back-door in social graphs.
Configurable reach
You decide whether discovery covers direct friends only or also friends of friends. Second-degree matches require both people to opt in, and the connecting friend is the only identity ever surfaced.
