Consent-first social discovery

Find your people,
right where you are.

Sometimes you just want a coffee or a break, without texting ten people to ask "who's around?". Project Proximity quietly tells you when someone you actually chose is nearby right now, and only when you both switch discovery on. No maps, no always-on tracking, no broadcasting where you are.

What makes it different

Built privacy-first, on purpose

Most location apps ask you to trust them with everything, all the time. We designed the opposite: the system only knows where you are for the few minutes you choose to be discoverable, and never in precise detail.

YOUR CIRCLE

You choose who counts

Your phonebook is not your friend list. You explicitly select who you want to see, and it stays mutual: they only learn about it if they choose you too.

EPHEMERAL

Nothing lingers

There is no permanent map of where you have been. A presence check-in lasts 15 minutes, then disappears from the system entirely.

REAL PRESENCE

Actually there, not spoofed

Instead of a raw GPS pin, the app uses nearby Wi-Fi signals to prove two people are genuinely in the same place, privately and hard to fake.

FRIENDS OF FRIENDS

Expand, safely

Opt in to a wider circle and a friend of a friend can appear as "Friend of Sam", with their name kept private. You meet through the person you both know.
